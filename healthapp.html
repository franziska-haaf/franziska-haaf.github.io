Healthapp

Submit your private health insurance documents digitally, quickly and easily.

Problem

Currently, there are different health apps for different clients that provide the same service. This leads to high maintenance and adaptation costs. A common app is now to be created for all existing and future customers - with individual customisation options. This will enable private health insurance companies to offer high-quality, user-centred digital document submission for their policyholders easily and cheaply.

Mission

To design a simple mobile app that enables insured persons with an average age of 64 to submit their documents digitally, easily and with as little assistance as possible.

Role

UX Designer

Timeline

June 2021 - October 2021

Methods

Qualitative interviews | Competitive analysis | Online questionnaire | Wireframes | Prototypes | Storyboard | Flow diagrams | High fidelity designs | Cognitive walkthrough | Heuristic evaluation | Usability testing

Tools

Figma | Balsamiq | Microsoft Teams | Procreate | Blender

About the Healthapp

The health app is a standardised app for private health insurance companies that enables them to offer document management for their users under iOS and Android without much effort on their part. The health insurance companies can have certain adjustments made, such as images, texts and different requirements for account creation. The health insurance companies then launch the app under their own name in the app stores.The new development of the uniform health app is currently taking place in cooperation with 2 health insurance companies as current customers. Further customers are planned.

The average age of those insured by private health insurance companies is 64 (as of 2015, source IGES Institut 'Beitragsentwicklung in der PKV'), which means that particular importance must be attached to accessibility and simple comprehensibility.

Research

Qualitative interviews with clerks

Qualitative interviews with employees of a private health insurance company who take care of the processing of submitted documents. It was important to make the stakeholders understand that I think from the user's perspective and do not want to visualise the (complicated) processes of the health insurance companies for the users.

I wanted to find out on how the clerks work, what the 'life cycle' of a document ( receipt, cost estimate, invoice, ...) is like. How the clerks react to queries from insured persons and problems. How long the processing normally takes. What technical options we have - what additional data can we get and send.

The most important finding was, that a users individual submissions within a certain time frame are assigned to an individual case worker and processed collectively by them. The insured person then receives a single settlement. These dependencies must be presented in the app in a meaningful and understandable way.

Competitive UX analysis

Competitive UX analysis of other German providers of health apps and apps that offer comparable services such as digital scanning of documents.

In-app survey

I created a short, easy-to-answer survey for in the apps (iOS and Android). Care needs to be taken when analysing the answers to the last question - when do users want to submit their documents - as the survey pops up as only as you start the app and the user can't be notified without opening the app manually. So a person who only submits at the end of the year would probably only open the app at the end of the year and not see this survey.

The answers (especially the requested functionalities) were subsequently evaluated together with the PO and the stakeholders as to their priority. The most requested feature was the'digital return channel', i.e. the possibility to receive the settlements from the health insurance company digitally via app. These are currently still sent to the users by post. In the future, the settlements will also be sent digitally via the app (when the users have given their explicit permission).

Conception

Storyboard

I created a short storyboard illustrating the cycle of a document from being received - for example, from a doctor - to being processed by the health insurance company.

Flow diagrams

Various flow diagrams have been created in consultation with PO and stakeholders to map processes such as registration, account verification, document scanning, document submission, document viewing, contact, etc.

Mockups

Creation of mock-ups. Comparative designs, processes, proposals, ideas and co. Evaluated with PO, DEVs and stakeholders and in some cases with end-users.

Designs

Based on the wireframes, high fidelity designs were created. Depending on the health insurance company, the primary and secondary colours are adapted and, if desired, own graphics are used.

I created a Design system and a styleguide based on Material in Figma. Platform-specific components for iOS and Android were taken into account.

Click prototype

With the high fidelity designs, a click prototype was created that almost entirely depicts the app. Registration, login, onboarding, verification, document cycle, profile adjustments, etc.

Tariff information

One feature that one of our customers would like to offer its insurants (and which, according to its own market research, is desired by the insurants) is to be able to view the personal tariffs and, if necessary, compare and change them. In the later user tests, we also found out that the users consider this feature to be positive, since they then do not have to 'search through the paper documents' to find out, for example, how much subsidy they get for glasses.

Since the tariffs are rather confusing at first glance, I have displayed the tariffs in flow charts and broken them down in order to better assess how they could be presented visually.

Based on this, I created wireframes, which were then tested with the users in the subsequent user tests.

Testing

Heuristic evaluation

I did heuristic evaluations of the existing apps as well as my own designs. I used 10 heuristics based on ISO 9241-110.

Cognitive walkthrough

Regular cognitive walkthroughs helped to ensure a good experience even with many adjustments.

Usability testing

For the usability testing, insured persons of one of our private health insurers were selected by an employee of the health insurance company and invited to the test. Care was taken to ensure that the target group was represented. The tests took place remotely due to the Corona pandemic. I participated as the interviewer as well as the PO and 2 stakeholders of the health insurance company. A questionnaire and tasks were prepared in advance, which the users carried out using the Figma High fidelity click prototype.

The evaluation of the usability tests was evaluated and documented as a usability test report and communicated to the team and stakeholders.

Accessibility

As the target group got an average age of 64, special care was taken with offering good accessibility and checked in the entire process. The developers were also briefed to use all the options available to them in the iOS and Android development. In the usertests, for example, it was found that our end-users have difficulty with pure icon-buttons and prefer to have text-buttons.

Currently, the app displays three steps with text + image as an introduction to the document scanning process. This means that a lot of support is needed from the employees in the current implementations. In the future, a different solution will be used, such as the (optional) possibility of showing overlays on the respective interfaces to explain which feature is where and what it does.

Possible implementation for a tutorial overlay explaining the scan process. This is only a design proposal and has not been tested further.
