TONIC.

A real time bidding platform to buy highly converting advertising traffic.

Problem

TONIC. is full of partly known usability problems, unfriendly for beginners and with an outdated design. The product was originally built with a tech focus and never designed with the user in mind.

Mission

Build a modern, competitive product that is oriented towards the user and supports them in their rapid, real-time critical work process in the best possible way.

Role

Senior UX Designer as an integrated part of the cross-functional Scrum team

Timeline

July 2021 - December 2021

Methods

Interviews | Personas | Web analysis | Card sorting | User Journey Map | Wireframes | Prototyping | Designs | Collaborative sketching | Heuristic evaluation | Usability testing

Tools

Figma | Adobe AfterEffects | Dropbox Paper | Jira | Confluence | Blender | Zoom | Procreate

About TONIC.

TONIC. is formally called 'TONIC. for Advertisers'. It is a platform that enables the purchase of the advertising types 'POP' and 'Domain Redirect traffic'. In the future, a third option will also be offered which includes preconfigured ads of the advertising types 'Search', 'Native' and 'Social'. POP, as the name suggests, is advertising in the form of pop-ups. Domain redirect traffic comes from landing pages that are displayed on parked domains.

TONIC. provides two interfaces: one for 'normal' users - the internal advertisers - and since recently one for feed advertisers (which are maintaining their campaigns via API but set up certain things from a small extra UI). In this context, we are talking about the user interface for the majority of the user group - the internal advertisers.

Users buy the existing traffic (POP and redirect) by creating and maintaining so-called 'campaigns'. Through the campaigns, you set the general conditions, for example, that you only want to buy clicks that fall under the area 'food' and come from mobile devices between 6 pm and 12 am because you want to sell pizza. The new ad type will offer preconfigured ads which will be purchasable via the so-called Marketplace in form of 'marketplace-offers'.

Research

As no research was ever conducted and an online questionnaire via newsletter remained largely unanswered, the users and their needs, wishes and problems are not really known. The account managers who are in contact with users know the biggest pain points, but no possibilities to address them.

Web analytics

We implemented "Matomo" for our current (and future) UIs for tracking user data in a way that is non-personal and data privacy compliant. Matomo gives us the opportunity to examine our site statistics in great detail. Which pages are visited for how long, with which end device, from which country, etc. Matomo has proven to be particularly valuable for analysing which currently implemented TONIC. Features are not used at all or rarely.

Qualitative interviews with sales

I started with qualitative interviews of our TONIC. sales people who are responsible for the users in form of account management and help desk. I wanted to learn how TONIC. does work, how users create and maintain campaigns and what their needs and problems are.

Qualitative interviews with users

I conducted user interviews with experienced users via Zoom. In doing so, I had developers from the team with me to take notes so that they also got in touch with the users. I used a series of guiding questions and had the users show me - via sharing their screen - how they work with TONIC. and where it is in their daily workflow.Â  This revealed that they generally do not spend much time on the TONIC website. For some users, for instance, they only login a few times a week. This is mainly due to the fact that the majority of users also use other (competing) products and the data of all products is fed into a combined tracking tool via API. When users log into TONIC, they do so to do things that their tracking tool does not offer them, such as creating new campaigns or adjusting bids and daily budgets.

User stories created from the user interviews. Clustered by topics and rated by frequency of mentioning.

Personas

I conducted a persona workshop with the account managers. The workshop was hold digitally via Miro. We used templates that included the persona demographics, needs and how these needs are served. We created 2 personas for Internal users (1 Media Buyer and 1 Affiliate) and 1 feed advertiser. The personas where verified via the user interviews later on.

Research findings

Besides asking why people use TONIC., what their goal is, how they work and where they know TONIC. from, we collected what they like, what they don't like and possibilities for improvement.

How users work
  • None of the interviewed users used TONIC.s internal tracking tool.
  • When publishing a campaign they start broad.
  • Features like source bidding or black-/and whitelisting are only used after the success of the campaign has been observed for a little while.
  • Users want to run 2 exactly identically configured campaigns with mobile and desktop in parallel in comparison
  • Naming conventions for campaigns.
  • Daily budget without spreading to watch how fast the budget is spent.
  • Visit TONIC. almost exclusively for creating and/or modifying campaigns
Bad bid suggestion

Users have complained that the bid suggestion does not work well. Users either increase the suggested bid or orientate themselves on the bid of existing, similar campaigns.

"It would make my life easier if the suggested bid would work."

Nontransparent source tracking
  • Source-IDs are hashes that represent the website from which a redirected user comes.
  • Only the general bid for the entire campaign is included in the tracking link, but not individually set source ID bids. To solve this, some users create completely new campaigns just for individual source IDs in order to better track the bids.
  • The list of set individual source ID bids can only be viewed in the edit view of a campaign or through tab navigation in the detailed view of a campaign.

"Source-ID tracking is a regular challenge. It's very laborious and gives me a headache."

Daily uniques
  • The daily uniques indicate the expected uniquer visitors per day for the campaign with the given configuration.
  • The daily uniques are "an important information when creating a campaign" but are only listed on page two of the campaign creation.
  • The daily uniques are very inaccurate and usually do not prove true. This makes them unsuitable for a trustworthy forecast.

"The daily uniques are not reality."

Bad folder feature
  • The feature to organise multiple campaigns in a common folder is not known and not used.
  • It is poorly implemented and not understandable to users.
  • The feature was not actively requested by the users and therefore does not seem to have a great need among the users.
Choosing keywords is hard
  • Users ask their account manager for advice on which keywords work best.
  • Keywords are added via a free text field which offers no input help.
  • A 'Keyword Finder', which can be used on demand, offers a search field via which suitable suggestions for existing keywords are offered for selection.
Open questions
Some questions arose that need deeper research:
  • The feature 'traffic estimator' has nearly no usage. Do we need this feature? Can we improve it?
  • Is the 'tablet' function next to 'mobile' and 'desktop' necessary?
  • Do people use the dashboard-tabs?
  • FAQ usage by people that have no account manager
  • Reports function not broadly used. Opportunity for improvement?

Conception

Card sorting

As the existing campaign creation process is complicated and not very user-friendly, yet our most important part of the product, I conducted a card sorting workshop with the TONIC.team. I wanted to rethink the campaign creation process. This workshop also led to a discussion on whether we needed all the objects and functions (blacklisting, whitelisting and source ID bidding) in the campaign creation process at all. I then analysed this in the user interviews.

Output from the card sorting workshop.

First version of the user journey map, created in the workshop

User journey map

Also with the TONIC. team I conducted a in office user journey map workshop. I digitalised the output and the user journey map got introduced to the account manager after the session for validation and second input. The user journey specially made clear that we have to look closer on the new user onboarding in the future. The journey was later adapted according to the user interviews and more details were added.

Wireframes & Designs

Iteratively (starting with wireframes), I created high fidelity designs that were validated through cognitive walkthroughs and design critiques. Our focus is on the desktop view as only 6% of our users access TONIC via a mobile device. User research has also confirmed our assumption that the need of our users to use TONIC. mobile is almost non-existent. Nevertheless, we want to make the mobile experience as good as possible and therefore offer Responsive Design.

Click-prototype

I created a click prototype with the designs. In doing so, I represented the most important processes - account creation, campaign creation, campaign detail view, campaign editing, etc. I also created alternative versions for comparison where relevant.Â  Details such as hover effects and animations through GIFs were also implemented to create as real an experience as possible. The prototype was later used in the user tests.

Collaborative sketching workshop

In addition to the new design for the existing product, an important feature is the implementation of a new ad type. I conducted a 'crazy 8' collaborative sketching workshop in which the scrum team, sales and stakeholders participated. In this, the first design ideas for the display of the inventory for the new ad type as well as the offer for individual offers were created. It turned out that the best way for the new ad type would be to completely decouple it from the existing ad types and therefore from the necessary campaign creation. The idea and the first designs for the TONIC. Marketplace and its Marketplace offers were born.

Testing

Heuristic evaluation

One of the first things I did when I joined the team was a heuristic evaluation. I used heuristics based on ISO 9241-110 for 10. Most of the problems found could be solved by redesigning the UI, but existing problems, especially bugs, were solved according to their priority.

Usability testing

Usability tests were carried out using the high-fidelity click prototype. The tests took place remotely via Zoom. In addition to me as the interviewer, one person took notes in each session. There were predefined questions and tasks. In conclusion, the interviewees were asked about their subjective perception of usability using the SUS (System Usability Scale).

The designs were very well received for the overall part, smaller feedback such as wording, the wish for a warning when leaving the campaign creation or the validation of individual source IDs when inserting a large list of source IDs into the blacklist or whitelist were made.

Evaluation of the System Usability Scale responses from users. Unfortunately, the testing could only be carried out with 4 users, as it is very difficult to find TONIC users who agreed to participate.

Guerilla testing: Marketplace

Based on the results of the collaborative sketching workshop for the new ad type, a click prototype with wireframes was created. It enables the comparison of the two workflows favoured in the workshop. First, the bidding on individual offers with a 2-column view and second through a popup. The prototype will be tested in guerrilla tests as a next step.

The end

Unfortunately, at the end of 2021, we received the news that our product TONIC. was to be integrated with the competing product of a sister company for reasons of corporate strategy. This happened shortly after the implementation of the first new designs. We had to stop our work and as a team we are very sad because we are convinced that with the redesign of TONIC we were going to provide our users with a new, user-oriented TONIC. that supports them even better in their workflow. TONIC. started under the name of 'DNTX' in 2012.
